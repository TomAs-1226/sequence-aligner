The score climbs as the alignment gets worse

Same two sequences, swept across every gap penalty. Watch the score and the number of gaps move in opposite directions. The honest setting is where the gaps settle down, not where the score is highest.

Is this alignment better than chance?

We shuffle sequence 2 many times and align each shuffle. If the real score sits far to the right of the shuffled pile, the match is real and not luck. Works best on hemoglobin or cytochrome c.
